Biography

Hey there! I’m Navid, an Applied Math Scientist passionate about Random Matrix Theory (RMT) and concentration of measures and their applications in Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and High Dimension Low Sample Size (HDLSS) data analysis.

I'm currently a Doctoral Candidate at Florida State University major in Financial mathematics, where I’m fortunate to be advised by Professor Alec Kercheval. Previously, I got my masters degree in applied mathematics from the University of Washington with focus in Computational Finance and Risk Management. Before that, I received my B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from Sharif University of Technology.

Outside of research, I enjoy traveling—whether it's exploring new cities or just experiencing different cultures and cuisines. I'm also passionate about sports, with tennis being my favorite, but I’ll never say no to a good workout or a friendly match of any kind.

When I wind down, I play the occasional game of chess, backgammon or get lost in books—especially those on philosophy. I also have a growing interest in finance and investing.
